##  **Report Overview**

This project provides an end-to-end analysis of **Databel Telecommunications‚Äô customer churn**, usage behavior, demographic patterns, and service experience.  

Using **Excel, MySQL, Python, and Power BI**, the raw dataset was cleaned, explored, modeled, and transformed into an interactive dashboard that reveals the underlying factors behind customer churn.

The report highlights:
- Customer distribution across states  
- Churn volume & retention performance  
- Usage intensity (average monthly GB download)  
- Customer service call behavior  
- Demographic influences  
- Payment method distribution  

It supports **data-driven decision-making** on churn reduction and customer experience improvement.

## üîç **Key Insights**

###  **1. Customer Churn Overview**
- **1,796 churned customers** out of **6,687** ‚Üí **~27% churn rate**  
- Indicates significant retention concerns requiring focused strategy.

---

### üì° **2. Usage Behavior**
- Over **45,000 GB** downloaded monthly across customers.  
- Low-usage customers tend to make **more service calls**, suggesting dissatisfaction not tied to data usage.

---

###  **3. Customer Service Call Patterns**
- Total of **6,100+** service calls.  
- High service call frequency strongly correlates with churn.  
- Indicates possible service quality or onboarding issues.

---

###  **4. Geographic Performance**
States such as **WV, OH, AL, MT, MD, NY, and SC** show:
- High customer counts  
- Noticeable churn levels  

States with a high share of **Unlimited Plan** users show **lower churn**, pointing to stronger value perception.

---

###  **5. Payment Methods**
- Only **4 available payment methods**.  
- Limited payment flexibility may contribute to customer frustration or churn.

---

###  **6. Demographic Patterns**
- **Under 30** group contributes most to churn.  
- **Senior** customers show the highest loyalty.  
- ‚ÄúOthers‚Äù group forms the majority of total customers.

---

###  **7. Usage vs Account Length vs Complaints**
- Long-tenure customers make **fewer service calls** ‚Üí Higher satisfaction.  
- Newer customers (shorter account age) make **more complaints** ‚Üí Onboarding challenges.  
- Heavy data users tend to remain loyal ‚Üí They perceive more value.
